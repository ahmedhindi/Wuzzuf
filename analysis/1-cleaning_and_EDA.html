note:¶

since I don't have access to the user data I don't have a way of knowing whether that conclusion is true for 'relevant views'* or not since probably the more date you fill the most likely it is that relevant views will increase and that might increase in relevant job application.

and that's true for all conclusions and that's what makes it nearly impossible to make almost any "Actionable insights" from this data.

all this data can provide is an interesting but incomplete view of the whole picture.

for analysis to be actionable it has to be backed by statistical evidence, that is taking a certain action is favorable over other action. and that's not easy that requires testing hypothesis and experiential design in this case for example (A/B testing - analyzing the trends before and after adding some feature to the website) and there are many steps to go from data to an actionable conclusion based on causal or highly correlated directorial relationships.

(*)(the views of users who are really interested in the job or the users who have skills relative to the job descriptions)
